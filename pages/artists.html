Tom Jobim

(January 25, 1927 - December 8, 1994)

Tom Jobim was a Brazilian composer, pianist, guitarist, songwriter, arranger, and singer. Considered "the father of Bossa Nova", he is one of the great exponents of Brazilian music.

Buena Vista Social Club

(1996 - 2015)

Buena Vista Social Club is a Cuban ensemble created in 1996, based on the homonymous club from La Habana. It has been known worldwide thanks to many notorious musicians such as Ry Cooder, Compay Segundo, Omara Portuondo, Ibrahim Ferrer, Rubén González, Eliades Ochoa, Barbarito Torres and Orlando 'Cachaíto' López, among others.

Laura Canoura

(January 2, 1957)

Laura Canoura is an Uruguayan singer, composer and actress, one of the most important female artists in her country. She was part of the renown Uruguayan group Rumbo and has worked along with artists such as Jaime Roos, Fernando Cabrera, Mariana Ingold and Mauricio Ubal, among others. She has been the first Uruguayan woman to have won a Golden and Platinum status for her album Esa tristeza.

José Carbajal "El Sabalero"

(December 8, 1943 - October 21, 2010)

José Carbajal, known as "El Sabalero", was an Uruguayan singer, composer and guitarist. He is one of the greatest references in Uruguayan popular music.

Combo Camagüey

(1960 - 1990)

Combo Camagüey was an Uruguayan orchestra conducted by Armando Bia and integrated by Jorge Vallejo, Santiago “Chileno” Salas, “Paqui” Vignole, Alexis Buenseñor, Juan Verrone, Neldo Castro, Rodolfo Martínez y Victor Morín. It was one of the most important groups of the genre known as "Uruguayan tropical music".

Eduardo Mateo

(September 19, 1940 - May 16, 1990)

Eduardo Mateo was an Uruguayan composer, guitarist and percussionist. His music is a blend of Uruguayan, Bossa Nova, jazz, beat and even Indian music. He is even nowadays one of the most influential musicians from Río de la Plata.
